Shields Up! How to Meet New People in the Internet Age

  • 1

    You meet someone new in cyberspace, but you haven’t yet met them in person.

  • 2

    Finally, you decide to meet up “In Real Life”

  • 3

    Tell Mr or Mrs Right that for your mutual safety, that you want to register on JusticeOnTheWeb.org. Respectfully ask for a copy of their driver’s license, phone number, and email address. If they get scared at this point, great! You just weeded up someone who doesn’t deserve you. But most will be only too happy to co-operate. So, take that information and register them.

  • 4

    They will get an email from JOTW asking them to confirm their email address, and a text to confirm their phone number.

  • 5

    Now, following all the other rules of safe first meetings (link goes here) go meet up and have a great time.

  • 6

    Once they do, you will get an email saying that they have been successfully registered.

  • 7

    But now, let’s say it didn’t go so well. You get stood up, or some other mishap happens. You now have an option: Document what occurred on JOTW.

  • 8

    Here is the magic: By the mere fact that they know you have a tool to document their misbehavior, they are going to think twice before they do you wrong.
